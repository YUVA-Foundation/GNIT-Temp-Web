First Year of Engineering____

First Year Engineering plays important role in building the foundation of an engineering graduate and learn basics of all branches along with Mathematics, Chemistry & Physics. We focus on the academic performance as well as industry interaction to develop overall personality of the students. The competent and enthusiastic faculty demonstrates unique team work for enhancing the academic and industrial knowledge among the students.

Overall development of the students we conduct various co-curricular & extra-curricular activities, effective planning, organizing, coordinating and teaching learning process. The department has well-furnished class rooms, laboratories and necessary infrastructural facilities Applied Sciences are the base of Engineering. The Department of First Year Engineering Department aims to help students develop strong fundamentals in all basic courses. With sound fundamentals, learning advanced and specialized courses become easier for students. Applied Physics, Applied Chemistry, Applied Mathematics, Communication Skills and ICC are the subjects taught at different stages of the four year course. The Department has a team of committed faculty members with proven skills in their respective fields and state-of-the-art Information in this entire Subject.

Applied Physics and Applied Chemistry are taught at first year level. The laboratories are well equipped to carry various analysis and conduct advanced practical in the field of Solid State Physics and Optics. Applied Mathematics, the core subject of Engineering, is taught up to pre-final year level. The subject helps students learn methods in Mathematical Modeling such as Classical, Numerical and Optimization techniques. The students are trained to incorporate mathematical models in Engineering situations and find plausible solutions for the same.

Department of First Year Engineering is also actively involved in pursuing research activities. The Department has the distinction of publishing and presenting maximum number of technical papers in peer reviewed national, international journals and conferences.
